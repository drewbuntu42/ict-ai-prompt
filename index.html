The Product & Tool

Portia is the AI coordination layer that sits between Wichita's existing organizations — a shared intake router, real-time bed registry, and case triage engine.

Meet Portia — Your Personal Navigator

A multilingual AI assistant that meets people where they are, builds a secure profile, and connects them to exactly the right services — no wrong answers, no wrong door.

STEP 01 — CONVERSATION
Talk in Any Language

Portia opens a natural-language conversation in the user's preferred language. She asks for name, date of birth, current situation, and immediate needs — adapting her questions based on each answer.

STEP 02 — PROFILE + FORMS
Fill Forms Automatically

Once Portia has the user's information, she offers to auto-fill applications for relevant shelters, programs, and services. The user reviews and approves. One conversation does the work of a dozen intake forms.

STEP 03 — YOUR CARD
A Physical Key to Everything

Profile saved securely. User visits any partner municipal site to receive a weather-proof plastic card with a QR code linked to their profile. They set their own PIN — 2FA built in.

How the Decision Tree Works

🌳

Adaptive Questioning

Portia follows a structured decision tree that branches based on each response. A foster youth aging out of DCF gets different questions than someone experiencing a first housing crisis — and different resources.

🌐

Multilingual by Default

Full support for Spanish, Vietnamese, Somali, and other languages common in Wichita's unhoused population. Language is detected automatically or chosen at first prompt. No interpreter required.

📋

Live Resource Updates

Portia knows what's available right now. If a shelter has moved, closed, or reached capacity, she knows. No more sending someone across town to a full building or a relocated address.

The Card System — Portia in Your Pocket

🪪 The Portia Card

A weatherproof, plastic ID-sized card issued at any partner municipal site — City Hall, Open Door, the public library. It has one thing on it: a QR code.

Scanning the QR code opens Portia, who asks for the user's PIN. Once authenticated, Portia loads their saved profile and picks up the conversation where they left off — surfacing what's changed, what's new, and what they need to do next.

Weather-proof plastic QR + PIN (2FA) Issued same-day

🚨 The Hermes Card — Emergency Medical

A second card, issued optionally, carries emergency medical information. When scanned by EMS or an ER nurse, it opens Hermes — a separate, read-only AI assistant designed for first responders.

Hermes surfaces medications, allergies, conditions, emergency contacts, and any special instructions — without requiring the user to be conscious or able to communicate. No PIN required for the medical track.

EMS / ER access No PIN (read-only) User-controlled content

⚠ Emergency Response Protocol — System Status: Critical

Portia Emergency Mode

1:00 PM — system-status endpoint flipped to EMERGENCY. Portia detected the crisis automatically and activated the following surge response.

🔴 Critical — Open Door Offline

Highest-volume walk-in hub offline 48–72 hrs. Portia immediately reroutes all Open Door intake to H.O.T. mobile units and CrossRoads overflow. H.O.T. second van deployed to Open Door's physical location.

🔴 Critical — 60+ Displaced Incoming

Severe weather event. 60 residents flooding the shelter network. 211 Kansas overloaded. Portia activates pre-authorized surge partners: church overflow network, YMCA emergency beds.

⚠ Warning — CrossRoads at 1/12 Beds

CrossRoads operating at extreme capacity. Portia suspends standard CrossRoads referrals immediately. All new adult intakes routed to surge overflow sites only.

⚠ Warning — 4 Foster Youth, 72-hr Window

DCF IL issuing 4 youth within 72 hours. BRIDGES has zero vacancy. Portia escalates to Second Light emergency intake track and authorizes Sedgwick County emergency housing vouchers.

ℹ BRIDGES — Zero Vacancy

BRIDGES case manager notified. 4 DCF youth placed on priority waitlist. Second Light contacted — 2 emergency beds potentially available pending same-day confirmation.

ℹ 211 Kansas — Saturated

Portia's real-time capacity feed becomes 211's primary routing source. Portia SMS line (316-PORTIA) activated as direct intake bypass while 211 call volume peaks.

Immediate Actions — Next 4 Hours
  • Activate Surge Overflow Network
    Contact pre-authorized overflow partners (Central Christian, First Presbyterian, YMCA Emergency Program). Estimated surge capacity: 45–60 additional beds within 2 hours.
  • Deploy H.O.T. as Primary Intake Hub
    H.O.T. mobile units become de facto intake while Open Door is offline. Second H.O.T. van deployed to Open Door's usual location. All H.O.T. intake flows through Portia digitally.
  • Activate Foster Youth Emergency Track
    DCF IL case worker contacted directly. Second Light emergency beds reserved for 2 of 4 youth. NEXTenant youth rapid-rehousing funds authorized for remaining 2 — hotel bridge if needed.
  • Push Live Capacity Feed to 211
    Portia API feeds real-time bed availability to 211 Kansas. Callers get current, accurate placement options instead of outdated lists, reducing call volume from bad referrals.
  • Notify All Partner Case Managers
    Automated push notifications to all active case managers: current capacity snapshot, rerouting instructions, emergency protocol PDF. Response acknowledgement tracked in Portia dashboard.
  • Log All Emergency Diversions
    Every emergency placement, diversion, and unmet need logged in Portia's database in real time. Creates after-action report for City, county officials, and funder review.
